Introduction • Screen 7 of 9

How You Will Learn

Six support tools are built into the lessons of this credential. Each serves a specific purpose within the self-paced flow. You do not need to locate them manually -- they appear at the appropriate moment within each lesson.

Reflection Checkpoints

A brief prompt at the end of most lessons inviting you to connect what you just learned to your own practice or a design project you have worked on. Responses are not collected or evaluated.

ARCH Decision Logs

A running record of every significant design decision made in the Meridian scenario, including the rationale and the alternative that was ruled out. The log accumulates across all four modules.

Learning Science Decision Logs

Notes on how each ARCH step is grounded in research on how people learn. These appear in selected lessons and explain the evidence basis for the method's design choices.

Governance Decision Logs

Formal records of decisions made at the governance gates built into the ARCH Method. These are the decisions that become part of the final Structural Freeze Declaration Package.

Calibration Activities

Short self-check activities that appear before or after key concepts. These are for your own calibration only and do not contribute to your credential evaluation.

Governance Checkpoints

Formal gates at the end of each module where you review your artifact against a governance checklist before advancing. A checkpoint must be cleared before you can proceed to the next module.

You will encounter each tool for the first time in Module 1. By Module 4, you will have used all six across the full ARCH workflow.
