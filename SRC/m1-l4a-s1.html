Before You Begin

What is the difference between Domain Authenticity calibration and Taxonomy Calibration?

Saved

Reference answer

Domain Authenticity calibration checks whether each scope item reflects real knowledge demands from the performance context. Taxonomy Calibration assigns the correct Bloom's level to each scope item based on what the performance actually requires.

What Steps 5 and 6 Do

5 Phase 1 Validate Macro Performance Arc Step 5

"What should a learner be able to do at the end of this course that they cannot do now?"

The macro performance arc is the explicit statement of end-of-course competence: what fully capable performance looks like for an MRHN healthcare IT professional who completes this course. Step 5 does not create the arc from scratch: the scope items, bucket structure, and calibration already implied it. Step 5 makes the arc explicit, validates it against the constraint set, and confirms it is coherent, authentic, and feasible before decomposition begins.

The arc operates at two levels: a macro arc covering the full course, and four bucket-level sub-arcs, one per bucket, that describe the trajectory of competence within each workflow phase.

Produces: MRHN_PerformanceArc_v1
6 Phase 2 Decompose Scope Items into Subcomponents Step 6

"What are the discrete, teachable units that build toward the performance arc?"

Step 6 breaks each of the 16 scope items into discrete decomposition components (DC components): the units that Module 2 will engineer into instruction sequences, activities, and assessments. Each DC component is a learnable unit: specific enough to be taught and assessed independently, bounded enough to fit within a coherent lesson.

Calibration annotations from Step 4 govern every component. A scope item calibrated at Analysis level produces DC components requiring analytical practice, not procedure-following activities. A domain authenticity flag on the parent item produces a component-level constraint on content. Step 4 work travels into every decomposition decision in Step 6.

Produces: MRHN_DecompositionMap_v1 (DC-01 through DC-48)

Why the Arc Precedes Decomposition

The performance arc sets the ceiling. Decomposition builds toward it. If you decompose before validating the arc, you risk producing DC components that collectively fall short of genuine competence: because no one checked what genuine competence actually requires.

The arc also prevents over-decomposition. Not every sub-skill that could be taught needs to be taught. Step 5 establishes which capabilities a healthcare IT professional at MRHN must be able to exercise independently at end of course, and which capabilities they may rely on colleagues, tools, or established procedures to provide. Decomposition in Step 6 is bounded by that distinction: only capabilities that belong in the arc get DC components.

Step 5 is also the last point at which feasibility can be checked before significant decomposition effort is invested. If the arc as stated cannot be achieved within the constraint set (LC-1 scope, PC-3 through PC-5 authority, RC-1 through RC-3 documentation requirements), it must be adjusted here, not discovered as a gap in Module 3 stress-testing.
