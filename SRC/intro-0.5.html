Introduction • Screen 5 of 9

The Gradual Release Model

This credential uses a cognitive apprenticeship structure to sequence support across four modules. In Module 1, you work alongside expert modeling and receive structured feedback on every decision. By Module 4, the scaffolding is gone. You apply the ARCH Method™ independently, with calibration-based feedback on your work. This pattern is the Gradual Release of Responsibility. Support decreases as your competence with the method grows.

Scroll right to view the full chart.

The arc follows four phases of the cognitive apprenticeship model. Each module maps to one phase in that arc.

  • Module 1
    Modeling and Coaching

    Worked examples walk through every ARCH decision with expert think-aloud narration. Guided practice provides immediate, criterion-referenced feedback. Support is at its highest.

  • Module 2
    Coaching

    Worked examples continue but require more active judgment from you. Decision point prompts supply criteria without modeling the answer. Support moderates as fluency builds.

  • Module 3
    Scaffolding Withdrawal

    Expert narration decreases. You apply diagnostic criteria to your own Meridian architecture with limited guidance and compare your judgment to expert benchmarks.

  • Module 4
    Fading

    Instructional scaffolding is minimal. You complete the MRHN Architecture Package through independent judgment. Feedback is calibration-based rather than instructional.

The course tools, including the glossary, decision history, and support panel, stay available in all four modules. Only the instructional scaffolding withdraws.
