Identifying Your Binding Constraint

Reflection Prompt

You have read the Meridian brief, watched an expert extract design constraints, and written your own scope boundary statement. Now make one choice that matters. Identify the single constraint that anchors everything else. That one constraint will guide every architecture decision you make for the rest of this credential.

  1. Which single constraint from the Meridian brief matters most to you as the designer? Explain what you chose and what it controls, not just what it is.
  2. How will that constraint shape your architecture decisions in Modules 2, 3, and 4? Name at least two specific decision points where it will have visible impact.

Writing Guidance

  • Name the constraint precisely as the brief states it, not a category or paraphrase. "Four hours of SME access with the IR team lead" is specific. "Limited SME time" is not.
  • Focus on what the constraint controls. A constraint that limits your validation time also limits how much scope revision you can afford. It limits how late you can catch errors.
  • When you write about Modules 2 through 4, name concrete decision types: sequencing decisions, instructional strategy choices, stress-testing priorities. Not general statements about being "mindful" of the constraint.
  • There is no single correct answer. Multiple constraints could legitimately be named as the most important. What matters is the quality of your reasoning, not the constraint you pick.

Name the specific constraint from the Meridian brief and explain what it controls: what becomes limited or visible because of it.
