Lesson 1.4b -- Final Screen

Assumptions Under Pressure: What the Audit Work Revealed

Reflection Prompt

Every practitioner enters decomposition with working assumptions about how scope items behave -- how many components they generate, whether components can always be sized independently, whether tracing is a formality or a genuine quality check. This lesson was designed to put those assumptions under observable pressure. Before you close Module 1, name one and account for what happened to it.

  1. The assumption: Identify one assumption you held about decomposition -- component sizing, traceability, scope coverage, or audit rigor -- before working through the optimization or audit practice in this lesson. State it precisely enough that another practitioner would recognize it as a real position, not a vague intuition.
  2. What the work revealed: Did the optimization decisions (Steps 4 and 5) or the trace audit (Steps 2-4 of Screen 4) challenge or confirm that assumption? Name the specific moment -- a verdict, a correction, a calibration note -- that made the assumption visible. Then state what, if anything, changes in your approach going forward.

Writing Guidance

  • Name the assumption precisely -- not "I thought decomposition was hard" but "I assumed every scope item would map to exactly two components at matched taxonomy levels."
  • Connect your assumption to a specific practice item from Screens 2-4: which component, which verdict, which trace result made the assumption visible?
  • Confirmed assumptions are as informative as challenged ones. If the work confirmed what you expected, explain what evidence confirmed it and whether your confidence in the assumption increased.
  • State a concrete change: not "I will be more careful" but "I will run the Q1 directional test before assigning a count, not after."

Gate 1.TG.1 Module 1 Threshold Check

Gate 1.TG.1 is the readiness check at the end of Module 1. It is not a scored test. It is a structured review of your governance record log -- a check that your eight records are complete, internally consistent, and coherent with one another before you advance to Module 2. At the gate, you will confirm that MRHN_DecompositionMap_v2 accurately reflects all optimization and audit decisions made across Lessons 1.4a and 1.4b, and that no scope items or components were added outside LC-1 scope. If any record is missing or contradicted by a later decision, the gate asks you to resolve it before proceeding. Passing the gate means your structural design is ready to receive the learning engineering work that begins in Module 2.
