Introduction • Screen 3 of 9

The Meridian Scenario

Every decision point, worked example, and performance task in this credential uses the same organizational context: Meridian Regional Health Network. One scenario across all four modules lets you practice the ARCH Method™ on a realistic design problem without switching contexts. You will build the Meridian course architecture step by step as you work through each Phase and Step of the method.

The Organization

Meridian Regional Health Network operates six acute-care hospitals and a network of outpatient clinics across a three-state region, with approximately 4,200 employees. In late 2023, a ransomware attack disrupted electronic health record access at two of its facilities for eleven days. A post-incident review identified a critical capability gap: IT staff lacked a shared, documented protocol for detecting, containing, and reporting cybersecurity incidents. Meridian's Chief Information Security Officer commissioned a formal training program to close that gap before the next incident cycle.

Project Brief
Course Title
Cybersecurity Incident Response for Healthcare IT Professionals
Organization
Meridian Regional Health Network
Learner Population
Approximately 120 healthcare IT professionals across six facilities: help desk technicians, network engineers, and systems administrators with mixed experience levels in cybersecurity
Regulatory Context
HIPAA breach notification requirements and Meridian Incident Response Policy IR-2024
Your Role
Instructional designer hired to build the course architecture before content development begins
Client Contact
Priya Okonkwo, Director of IT Education and Workforce Development

You are not expected to be a cybersecurity expert or a healthcare professional. The ARCH Method™ operates at the level of course architecture, not subject-matter expertise. Meridian's subject-matter experts will develop the content. Your job is to build an architecture that makes that content development purposeful and defensible. The distinction between the architect's role and the developer's role is one of the core arguments the ARCH Method™ makes.

Why This Scenario

Healthcare is not a higher education context, and that distance is intentional. The ARCH Method™ is domain-agnostic: the same Phases and Steps apply whether you are designing a clinical training, an onboarding program, or a graduate seminar. Using a scenario outside your typical domain asks you to engage with the method as method rather than relying on context familiarity.

The Meridian scenario was chosen because it has real regulatory constraints, a defined but heterogeneous learner population, high-stakes performance requirements, and a clear organizational trigger that explains why the training must exist. Those features create the conditions where course architecture decisions have genuine consequences. That is exactly where the ARCH Method™ is most valuable.
