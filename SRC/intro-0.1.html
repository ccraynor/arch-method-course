Introduction • Screen 1 of 9

Welcome to the ARCH Method Practitioner Credential

Purpose

The ARCH Method is a constraint-governed workflow for course architecture design. Before you begin the four core modules, this nine-screen introduction orients you to the method, the course structure, and the tools you will use throughout. It also introduces Meridian Regional Health Network, the scenario that anchors every decision point, worked example, and performance task in this credential.

What You Will Do

  • Review how the ARCH Method compares to other course design frameworks you may already use
  • Explore the four-module structure and the performance tasks that benchmark your progress
  • Understand the Gradual Release model that governs how support is structured and withdrawn across the credential
  • Learn how to use the support tools, artifact drawer, and decision history built into this course environment
  • Write a Learner Commitment Note that sets your goals and working timeline for this credential

What You Will Produce

  • Learner Commitment Note documenting your goals, professional context, and working timeline for this credential (completed in Screen 0.9)
