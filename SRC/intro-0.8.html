Introduction • Screen 8 of 9

What to Expect

Before you begin Module 1, here is an accurate picture of what this credential requires. Each point below describes something that distinguishes this credential from a typical online course. Read these carefully before continuing to the commitment screen.

  1. 1

    Time Commitment: 22 to 26 Hours

    This credential takes 22 to 26 hours to complete across all four modules. Most learners budget 5 to 7 hours per module. The sessions do not need to be consecutive, but each module is designed to be completed in sequence without extended gaps between lessons. Interrupting a module mid-lesson and returning days later typically requires reorientation time.

  2. 2

    No Multiple Choice -- Artifact Only

    There are no multiple choice questions, quizzes, or fill-in exercises in this credential. Every module closes with a performance task that produces a design artifact applied to the Meridian scenario. Your credential is earned through the quality of what you design, not through test scores. This means the work is harder to fake and harder to rush.

  3. 3

    No Instructor Feedback

    This credential is fully self-directed. No instructor reviews your work or provides individual feedback. The rubric, the ARCH Decision Logs, and the Governance Checkpoints built into each module serve as your feedback system. By the time you reach the final evaluation, you will have a clear picture of where your work stands without waiting on anyone else.

  4. 4

    Self-Assessment Through Calibration

    Calibration activities throughout the lessons ask you to check your understanding against a reference response. You evaluate your own answer. These activities are the primary mechanism for checking comprehension between performance tasks. They work only if you engage with them honestly. The credential cannot enforce accurate self-assessment -- it depends on the seriousness you bring to the work.

  5. 5

    Professional-Level Rigor

    This credential asks you to work at the level of a practitioner. The Meridian scenario is realistic, the decisions you face are non-trivial, and the artifacts you produce are the same type a working instructional designer would create for an actual client. The credential does not simplify the work. It provides a structured workflow and a realistic scenario to scaffold your path through it.

The Bottom Line

If you bring the time and the intention to engage seriously, you will work through a complete ARCH workflow on a realistic scenario and produce design artifacts that demonstrate genuine professional competence in course architecture. That is what this credential is designed to deliver. The next screen gives you the opportunity to record your commitment before you begin Module 1.
