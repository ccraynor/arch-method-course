Introduction • Screen 8 of 9

What to Expect

Before you begin Module 1, here is an accurate picture of what this credential requires. Each point below sets this credential apart from a typical online course. Read these carefully before you continue to the commitment screen.

  1. 1

    Time Commitment: 22 to 26 Hours

    This credential takes 22 to 26 hours to complete across all four modules. Most learners budget 5 to 7 hours per module. Your sessions do not need to be consecutive. Still, complete each module in sequence without long gaps between lessons. If you stop mid-lesson and return days later, you will usually need time to reorient.

  2. 2

    No Multiple Choice: Artifact Only

    This credential has no multiple choice questions, quizzes, or fill-in exercises. Every module closes with a performance task that produces a design artifact applied to the Meridian scenario. You earn the credential through the quality of what you design, not through test scores. This makes the work harder to fake and harder to rush.

  3. 3

    No Instructor Feedback

    This credential is fully self-directed. No instructor reviews your work or gives you individual feedback. The rubric, the ARCH Decision Logs, and the Governance Checkpoints built into each module serve as your feedback system. By the time you reach the final evaluation, you will see clearly where your work stands without waiting on anyone else.

  4. 4

    Self-Assessment Through Calibration

    Calibration activities throughout the lessons ask you to check your understanding against a reference response. You evaluate your own answer. These activities are the main way you check comprehension between performance tasks. They work only if you engage with them honestly. The credential cannot enforce accurate self-assessment. It depends on the seriousness you bring to the work.

  5. 5

    Professional-Level Rigor

    This credential asks you to work at the level of a practitioner. The Meridian scenario is realistic, and the decisions you face are non-trivial. The artifacts you produce are the same type a working instructional designer would create for an actual client. The credential does not simplify the work. It gives you a structured workflow and a realistic scenario to scaffold your path through it.

The Bottom Line

Bring the time and the intention to engage seriously. In return, you will work through a complete ARCH workflow on a realistic scenario. You will produce design artifacts that show genuine professional competence in course architecture. That is what this credential is designed to deliver.
