Course Design Request Meridian Regional Health Network
From: Priya Okonkwo, Director of Workforce Learning, Meridian Regional Health Network
Date: September 15, 2024
Re: Course Design Request -- Cybersecurity Incident Response for Healthcare IT Professionals

Background

In Q3 2024, Meridian Regional Health Network experienced a ransomware intrusion that partially encrypted systems at our main hospital campus. The incident was contained after 14 hours. Post-incident review found that IT staff could not execute the documented incident response protocol (IR-2024) without supervisor escalation for every decision. Staff also could not make appropriate isolation calls within their role authority or communicate status to clinical leadership without scripted guidance.

An OCR audit in 2023 had already recommended documented improvement to incident response workforce training within 18 months. This project addresses both the internal gap and the compliance timeline.

Course Request

Meridian requests the design and development of a self-paced online course in Cybersecurity Incident Response for healthcare IT professionals. This is not a compliance checkbox course. We need staff who can act independently and correctly during a cyber incident.

Target Learners

  • Approximately 120 healthcare IT professionals across Meridian's three sites (main hospital campus plus two regional clinics)
  • Roles: systems administrators, network engineers, help desk staff, and IT managers
  • Prior knowledge: general IT competency; limited formal incident response training; all completed a basic HIPAA awareness module in 2022
  • Technology access: all learners have LMS access and complete training on company-managed devices

Required Performance

By the end of training, IT staff must be able to:

  1. Recognize early indicators of a cybersecurity incident and initiate the IR-2024 protocol without supervisor prompting
  2. Execute Tier 1 and Tier 2 IR-2024 protocol decisions independently, within their role authority
  3. Make appropriate system isolation decisions without escalating every call to a supervisor
  4. Document incident activity accurately and in real time in the SIEM event log
  5. Communicate incident status to clinical leadership using the approved Meridian communication templates

Regulatory and Compliance Context

  • HIPAA Security Rule (45 CFR 164.308(a)(5)) -- workforce training requirement for covered entities
  • IR-2024 -- Meridian's internal incident response protocol, adopted January 2024; currently active; the course must align to this protocol, not to generic frameworks
  • OCR audit finding (2023) -- recommended documented improvement to incident response workforce training within 18 months of the audit date

Project Constraints

Budget: Fixed; no travel, no in-person delivery Timeline: Delivery-ready within 90 days of project start Format: Self-paced online; SCORM 1.2 for LMS deployment; estimated learner-facing time 4 to 6 hours SME Access: Limited to 4 hours total with Meridian's IR team lead; schedule set in advance Existing Content: IR-2024 protocol document (full text provided); no prior course content or existing training materials

Deliverable Request

Before content development begins, provide a course architecture document for Meridian review. We require sign-off on scope and sequence before any content is written. The architecture document should include a defined scope, learning sequence, and alignment between required performance and planned instruction.
