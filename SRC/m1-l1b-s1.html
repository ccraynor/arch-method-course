Constraint Identification and Prioritization

Phase 1: Bound the Course Step 1: Design Document Synthesis Step 1.5: Output Style Gate and Snapshot Protocol

This lesson covers two consecutive steps. Step 1 produces the constraint map. Step 1.5 establishes the documentation conventions that all subsequent MRHN artifacts must follow. Both steps are completed before you leave this lesson.

Before You Begin

What is the single binding constraint from the Meridian brief that most limits your design options and why?

Saved

Reference answer

The 4-hour SME access ceiling is the most binding constraint. It is binding because it cannot be negotiated. It is a fixed institutional condition that directly limits the depth and number of components you can develop with expert validation.

Connect to What You Know

Lesson 1 established your scope boundaries for the Meridian project. This lesson extracts every constraint that brief contains and locks the documentation conventions you will use for the rest of the credential.

Purpose

Scope declared in Lesson 1.1a is boundary-setting. Constraint identification is diagnostic. It surfaces the non-negotiable conditions in your source documents that must govern every architecture decision you make downstream. Step 1 extracts and ranks those conditions from the Meridian materials. Step 1.5 locks the naming and versioning conventions that make all MRHN artifacts reviewable, traceable, and consistent through Structural Freeze.

What You Will Do

  • Apply the Design Document Synthesis protocol to the Meridian project brief and IR-2024 regulatory context
  • Extract non-negotiable constraints and categorize them by constraint type: performance, regulatory, logistical, and stakeholder
  • Rank constraints by their impact on architecture decisions, separating binding constraints from negotiable preferences
  • Apply the Output Style Gate to establish artifact naming, versioning, and snapshot conventions for all MRHN deliverables
  • Produce the first named artifact of the credential

What You Will Produce

  • MRHN Bucket Map and Scope Organization Package -- a structured constraint map organized by type and ranked by design impact, with your scope declaration attached and the Output Style Gate conventions documented
