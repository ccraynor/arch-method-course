Meridian Project Intake Review

Learning Objective

By the end of this lesson, you will read a project brief the way an expert does, extracting the constraints that govern every design decision that follows.

Phase 1: Bound the Course Step 0: Declare Locked Scope
Your Commitment

Purpose

The ARCH Method™ begins with reading, not designing. Before you declare a scope or make any architectural decision, you need to understand the project you have been handed. This lesson presents the Meridian project intake package. You will study the project brief, learner profile, and constraint documentation from Priya Okonkwo. Step 0 asks you to review this material. Then you confirm you have enough to proceed to scope declaration.

Constraint-governed design begins with reading, not with building.

What You Will Do

  • Read the Meridian project brief submitted by Priya Okonkwo at Meridian Regional Health Network
  • Review the learner population profile and the stated on-the-job performance context
  • Identify the regulatory and institutional constraints that will govern the course design
  • Assess the completeness of the intake package before proceeding to scope declaration in Lesson 2

What You Will Produce

This is a review and orientation lesson. You produce no MRHN-named artifact here. Your intake review feeds directly into the scope declaration in Lesson 2. That lesson produces the first component of your Scope Organization Package.
