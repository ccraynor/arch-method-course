Introduction • Screen 4 of 9

Credential Structure

This credential assesses four competencies through four cumulative performance tasks, one at the end of each module. Each task builds on the work you completed in the previous module. By the final task, you will have produced a complete course architecture for Meridian using every Phase and Step of the ARCH Method™.

Four Competencies

The credential recognizes competence in four areas of course architecture practice. Each competency is assessed across all four modules, with your performance tasks as the primary evidence.

Competency 1

Course Architecture Development

Design a complete course architecture from initial constraints through scope definition and learning design, applying each Phase and Step of the ARCH Method™ in sequence.

Competency 2

Instructional Sequence Engineering

Engineer a learning sequence that aligns identified outcomes, learning activities, and assessment tasks with the documented performance requirements and learner characteristics.

Competency 3

Structural Diagnostics and Design Integrity

Conduct stress tests on a working course architecture to identify structural weaknesses, scope drift, and outcome misalignment before content development begins.

Competency 4

Design Governance and Decision Traceability

Document design decisions with rationale at each Phase, maintain a complete decision trail, and produce a governance-frozen architecture ready for handoff to content development.

Performance Tasks

Each module closes with a performance task applied to the Meridian scenario. Tasks are cumulative: the artifact you produce in Module 1 becomes the foundation for Module 2, and so on through Module 4.

  1. Module 1

    Bucket Map and Scope Organization Package

    Map Meridian's course content into instructional buckets and organize the full scope into a structured package. This artifact anchors all design decisions in Modules 2 through 4.

  2. Module 2

    Sequenced Unit Architecture Package

    Extend your scope work into a sequenced unit architecture with decomposed outcomes and aligned assessments organized across the course structure.

  3. Module 3

    Audit and Governance Package

    Apply structural audits to your Module 2 architecture, identify design integrity issues, and produce a governance record of all findings and corrective decisions.

  4. Module 4

    Structural Freeze Declaration Package

    Apply the structural freeze protocol to your architecture and issue a formal declaration package with complete decision traceability, ready for handoff to content development.

Mastery Threshold

To receive this credential, your final performance tasks must meet the following threshold.

Mastery Threshold

The credential rubric uses three performance levels: Strong Evidence, Adequate Evidence, and Developing Evidence. The three criteria below require Strong Evidence at final evaluation. All remaining criteria must reach Developing Evidence or above.

  • Strong Evidence

    ARCH Workflow Application

    Applied each Phase and Step of the ARCH Method™ correctly and in the intended sequence across all four modules.

  • Strong Evidence

    Scope Fidelity

    Maintained consistency with the defined course scope from the Bucket Map and Scope Organization Package through the Structural Freeze Declaration Package.

  • Strong Evidence

    Governance and Traceability

    Documented design decisions with rationale at each Phase and produced a complete decision trail in the Structural Freeze Declaration Package.

Floor requirement: No criterion on the final evaluation may fall below Developing Evidence. Meeting the three gateway criteria above does not satisfy this threshold if any remaining criterion is below Developing Evidence.
