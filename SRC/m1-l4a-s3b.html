SI-09 Bucket 2 Analysis Calibration flag

Network Segmentation with Clinical System Interdependency

Calibration annotation (MRHN-D-003) Network segmentation at MRHN requires evaluating EHR, imaging, and billing system interdependencies before applying procedures. The patient care continuity constraint elevates this item to Analysis level. The evaluative work on clinical dependencies is what makes it non-procedural.
Bucket 2 arc constraint The phrase "without disrupting patient care at active MRHN facilities" must be reflected in any component that handles clinical interdependency evaluation.
What you know This scope item decomposes into three components: DC-19, DC-20, and DC-21. Because the calibration flag is set, at least two of the three must be Analysis level. The third is the procedural step.

Expert Decomposition: SI-09 (DC-19 through DC-21)

DC-19 Analysis

Evaluate clinical system interdependencies (EHR, imaging, billing) before applying network segmentation to determine which systems cannot be segmented without disrupting active patient care at the affected MRHN facility.

Assessment must present scenarios where segmentation would disrupt at least one clinical system. Learners must identify the conflict and evaluate the trade-off. A component whose assessment presents only clean segmentation scenarios does not meet the Analysis-level demand.

DC-20 Application

Apply the MRHN network segmentation procedure to isolate affected network segments while preserving continuity on identified patient-care-critical systems, following the approved segmentation sequence.

DC-21 Analysis

Document segmentation decisions including clinical systems evaluated, interdependencies identified, actions taken, and any segmentation deferred, naming the patient care factors that governed each deferral decision.

Documentation must name the care-continuity factors that drove deferral decisions, not just record what was done. A component whose documentation standard could be met by "checked clinical systems" is not Analysis-level.

Key insight

The calibration flag requires two Analysis-level components (DC-19 and DC-21). If a decomposition has only one Analysis-level component, or treats segmentation as a purely procedural task, the MRHN-D-003 constraint is not honored. The arc phrase "without disrupting patient care" must appear explicitly in at least one component.
